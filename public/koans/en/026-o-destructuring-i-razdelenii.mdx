# Koan №26 — "On Destructuring and the Division of the One"

<Source>Written in ink on a grain of rice found in the palm of the Amida Buddha statue, Kotoku-in temple, Kamakura, 1998 CE. A restorer read it under a magnifying glass. He wept. He quit. He became a frontend developer.</Source>

```js
const universe = { water: { depth: 'abyss', color: 'blue' } }
const { water } = universe
water.color = 'black'

universe.water.color  // 'black'
```

<Student>"Master, I took only the water from the universe."</Student>

<Master>"You took a copy of the name. The water itself — is the same."</Master>

<Action>The student turned pale.</Action>

<Master>"You thought you had separated the river from the world. But you took only its name. Touch it — and the world darkens."</Master>

<Student>"A shallow copy..."</Student>

<Master>"Shallow attachment. The depth — is shared."</Master>

<Haiku>The name apart. The river — the same. You touched it — the world darkened.</Haiku>

<Question>Destructuring copies primitives and references objects. Do you know what you hold in your hand — a copy or a leash?</Question>
