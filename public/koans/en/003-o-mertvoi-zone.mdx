# Koan №3 — "On the Dead Zone"

<Source>Found in the cellars of Todai-ji, 1309 CE. Monk Keisaku inscribed it on the back of the sutra "On Temporal Binding." The abbot was furious.</Source>

<Source>The student wrote confidently:</Source>

```js
console.log(x)
let x = 5
```

<Student>"Master, with var this worked. I received undefined and moved on."</Student>

<Master>"You moved on with emptiness in your hands and called it the path."</Master>

<Student>"Now — a ReferenceError. I am not allowed through."</Student>

<Master>"No. You have finally been stopped."</Master>

<Student>"But the variable exists! It is declared below!"</Student>

<Action>The master raised a cup of tea and held it over the floor.</Action>

<Master>"This cup is yours. I have already decided to give it. But until I release my fingers — reach out and take it."</Master>

<Action>The student reached out. The master struck his hand with a stick.</Action>

<Master>"Temporal Dead Zone. Knowing that a gift exists does not grant the right to take it before its time."</Master>

<Haiku>Seed in the earth — already a tree. Pull too soon — the roots tear.</Haiku>

<Question>var hoisted both name and value — but gave undefined, a quiet lie. let hoists the name, but forbids touching. Which is more merciful — the lie that does not break, or the truth that strikes?</Question>
