# Koan №33 — "On the Bridge No One Crossed"

<Action>Found in the ticket system of Horyu-ji monastery, 2026 CE. The ticket had been in "Code Review" status for 47 days. Priority — Critical. Master Unjyu saw it, closed the laptop and walked into the garden. He was found three hours later — carving into a stone: "A PR is not a gate. A mentor is not a gatekeeper. You are not the waiting."</Action>

<Action>The student sat by the wall. Not coding. Not reading. Sitting.</Action>

<Master>"Why do you not practice?"</Master>

<Student>"I am waiting. The mentor has not reviewed my PR. Fourth day."</Student>

<Master>"And?"</Master>

<Student>"I cannot move forward."</Student>

<Action>The master took a stick and drew a line in the earth.</Action>

<Master>"Here is your PR. Here is you. Here is the mentor. Between you — what?"</Master>

<Student>"Waiting."</Student>

<Action>The master erased the mentor.</Action>

<Master>"And now?"</Master>

<Student>"I... and my code."</Student>

<Master>"You and your code. That was always enough."</Master>

<Master>"While you wait for approval — read the code you already submitted. Find what you would do differently. Write the next task in a branch. Approval is a mark on the path. Not a gate."</Master>

<Haiku>The PR hangs. The mentor is silent. The path — is open.</Haiku>

<Question>A mentor who has not reviewed is not an obstacle. It is the silence in which you hear yourself. If you cannot move without someone else's approval — you depend not on the mentor, but on permission to grow. But who gave you that limitation — the process or yourself?</Question>
