# Koan №37 — "On the Great Replacement"

<Action>Projected by a neural network onto the wall of Kenninji temple, 2026 CE. The monks tried to wipe it off. The projection learned to dodge the cloth. Master Hideo unplugged the power cord. Said: "The first skill of a programmer."</Action>

<Action>The student did not come.</Action>

<Action>The master waited. One day. Two. On the third — found him in the garden without a laptop.</Action>

<Master>"Why do you not practice?"</Master>

<Student>"No point. AI has replaced programmers. I read it."</Student>

<Master>"Where?"</Master>

<Student>"An AI wrote the article about it."</Student>

<Action>The master sat beside him. Was silent.</Action>

<Master>"Did the calculator replace mathematicians?"</Master>

<Student>"No."</Student>

<Master>"Did autotune replace singers?"</Master>

<Student>"...no."</Student>

<Master>"The navigator broke in the mountains. Who found the way home — the one who knew the road, or the one who knew the app?"</Master>

<Action>The master lifted a beetle from a stone.</Action>

<Master>"AI generates code. As a river carries logs. But the river does not know what a house is. The builder knows — the one who understands wood."</Master>

<Haiku>The navigator is broken. The mountain — the same. The one who knew the road came home.</Haiku>

<Question>A tool amplifies the one who knows. In the hands of the ignorant — it amplifies ignorance. Do you hold the tool — or does the tool hold you?</Question>
