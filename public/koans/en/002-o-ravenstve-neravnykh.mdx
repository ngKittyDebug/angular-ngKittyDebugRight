# Koan №2 — "On the Equality of the Unequal"

<Source>From the charred parchment of Enryaku-ji temple, 1231 CE. Attributed to monk Rendan, commentator on Abstract Operations.</Source>

```js
[] == ![]   // true
```

<Student>"Master, an empty array equals the negation of an empty array. This is... impossible."</Student>

<Master>"You asked the language: 'are they equal?' The language began its work by the Abstract Equality Algorithm. It is not obliged to consider meaning."</Master>

<Student>"But [] is an object. ![] is false. How can they be equal?"</Student>

<Master>"Because you wrote ==. Not ===. You requested comparison with coercion. The language coerced. Do not complain about the fruit of a coercion you yourself ordered."</Master>

<Student>"Then === is always better?"</Student>

<Action>The master closed his eyes.</Action>

<Master>"Always — is the word of the presumptuous. But yes."</Master>

<Haiku>Empty bowl. Negation of an empty bowl. Both — on the table.</Haiku>

<Question>The language executes rules, not intentions. When you write ==, you sign a contract with the Abstract Equality Algorithm. Have you read it?</Question>
