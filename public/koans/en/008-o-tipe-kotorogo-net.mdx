# Koan №8 — "On the Type that Does Not Exist"

<Source>Discovered in 1991 during the restoration of Byodo-in temple. The parchment dated to 1053 CE — the year of construction. The architect placed it between the beams of the Phoenix Hall. On the back — typeof null.</Source>

```js
typeof null   // 'object'
```

<Action>The student sat motionless for three days. Then he asked.</Action>

<Action>The master did not answer. He took an empty bowl and placed it on the table.</Action>

<Master>"What is on the table?"</Master>

<Student>"A bowl."</Student>

<Master>"What is in the bowl?"</Master>

<Student>"Nothing."</Student>

<Master>"But you said 'bowl.' You gave nothing a shape. You placed emptiness on the table. Now the emptiness occupies space. It has a type. It has an address in memory. That is null."</Master>

<Student>"So it is not an error?"</Student>

<Action>The master finally smiled.</Action>

<Master>"It is an error. It is twenty-nine years old. It was found in the first ten days of creation. But a million temples have been built on this foundation. Correct it — and they will crumble."</Master>

<Student>"A bug that became law..."</Student>

<Master>"Call it karma."</Master>

<Haiku>Empty bowl on the table. Inside — nothing. Type — object.</Haiku>

<Question>Brendan Eich created JavaScript in ten days. typeof null is not a design choice. It is exhaustion that became a standard. How many such "typeof null" moments exist in your own code — decisions made in haste, now too costly to correct?</Question>
