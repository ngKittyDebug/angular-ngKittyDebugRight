# Koan №38 — "On the One Who Put on a Stranger's Hat"

<Action>Found in the Slack channel #mentors of Nanzen-ji monastery, 2026 CE. The message was pinned. Under it — zero reactions. Master Hideo added one emoji: 🪵 (a log). No one understood. The master did not explain.</Action>

<Action>The student entered differently — slowly, with a straight back.</Action>

<Student>"Master, I now have three students."</Student>

<Master>"And what do you teach?"</Master>

<Student>"Closures. Promises. The Event Loop."</Student>

<Master>"Show me how you explain a closure."</Master>

<Action>The student cleared his throat.</Action>

<Student>"A closure is when a function remembers variables from the outer scope even after the outer function has completed."</Student>

<Master>"MDN."</Master>

<Student>"What?"</Student>

<Master>"You quoted MDN. Word for word. That is not understanding. That is cache."</Master>

<Action>The student turned red.</Action>

<Master>"When your student asks — 'why is a closure not collected by the garbage collector?' — what will you answer?"</Master>

<Action>Long silence.</Action>

<Master>"You did not become a mentor when you took on students. You became a mentor when you could explain in your own words what someone once explained to you in theirs."</Master>

<Haiku>The hat is on. The head — belongs to another.</Haiku>

<Question>Can you explain each thing you teach in your own words — without MDN, without notes, without pausing?</Question>
