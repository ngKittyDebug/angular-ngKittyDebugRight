# Koan №35 — "On the Buddha on the Road"

<Action>From the ashes of a burned textbook "JavaScript: The Definitive Guide," Shoji-ji temple, date unknown. The master burned it himself. The student wept. The master said: "Now you will truly read it."</Action>

<Action>The student returned after a year.</Action>

<Student>"Master, I found the perfect framework."</Student>

<Master>"Kill it."</Master>

<Student>"I found the perfect mentor."</Student>

<Master>"Kill him."</Master>

<Student>"I found the perfect pattern that solves everything."</Student>

<Master>"Kill it."</Master>

<Student>"Then what should I keep?"</Student>

<Master>"console.log. An empty file. A task you do not know how to solve. And silence."</Master>

<Haiku>Met the Buddha — kill him. Met a framework — read the source.</Haiku>

<Question>While you seek authority — you do not learn, you believe. No teacher, course or framework will become your enlightenment. If you meet on the road one who knows everything — doubt them.</Question>
