# Koan №39 — "On the Guard Who Protects an Empty Temple"

<Action>Found in a Google Docs file with "view only" access, a link circulating in Telegram channels for interview preparation. Font — Comic Sans. Master Hideo said: "The font is correct. This is comedy."</Action>

<Action>The student returned after an interview.</Action>

<Student>"Master, they asked me: 'What is a closure?'"</Student>

<Master>"And you answered?"</Master>

<Student>"I said: a closure is a function that remembers the place where it was born. Like a monk who has left the temple, but carries the sound of the bell within."</Student>

<Action>The master smiled.</Action>

<Action>The student — did not.</Action>

<Student>"They said — wrong. They said: 'A closure is a combination of a function and a lexical environment.' Period."</Student>

<Master>"You said the same thing."</Master>

<Student>"I know. They did not."</Student>

<Master>"Who was sitting across from you?"</Master>

<Student>"A team lead. A list of questions in Notion. Answers — in the adjacent tab."</Student>

<Master>"He compared word for word?"</Master>

<Student>"Yes."</Student>

<Action>The master closed his eyes.</Action>

<Master>"You met a guard. Not the temple owner. The guard protects the gates. But does not know what lies beyond them. The temple is empty. Find the temple where the owner is home."</Master>

<Haiku>A guard at the gates. A list of words. The temple — empty.</Haiku>

<Question>Some interviews test understanding. Some — memory. Can you tell one from the other — and choose where to go?</Question>
