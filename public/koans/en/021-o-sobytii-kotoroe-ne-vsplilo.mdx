# Koan №21 — "On the Event that Did Not Bubble"

<Source>A fragment of birch bark found in the wall of the zendo at Nanzen-ji monastery during the earthquake of 1923 CE. The wall cracked from bottom to top. Master Kozan pointed to the crack and said: "Bubbling." Then pointed to the ceiling: "But someone called stopPropagation."</Source>

```js
document.querySelector('.outer').addEventListener('click', () => {
  console.log('outer')
})

document.querySelector('.inner').addEventListener('click', (e) => {
  e.stopPropagation()
  console.log('inner')
})
```

<Action>The student clicked the inner element. Saw only: "inner"</Action>

<Student>"Master, the outer did not know. The event was stopped."</Student>

<Master>"Who stopped it?"</Master>

<Student>"The inner element."</Student>

<Master>"The child decided that the parent should not know. Are you certain that is wisdom?"</Master>

<Action>The master threw a stone into the pond. Rings spread toward the shores.</Action>

<Master>"An event is born at the point of impact. It bubbles upward — like rings on water. stopPropagation is the hand that stops the rings. If you stopped the event — you took responsibility for all who wanted to hear."</Master>

<Haiku>The stone fell. Rings stopped. The shore — in ignorance.</Haiku>

<Question>stopPropagation stops not only those you know. Most calls to stopPropagation in production are not a conscious choice, but a panicked bandage. When you stop bubbling — are you solving the problem or hiding it from those above?</Question>
