# Koan №1 — "On the Emptiness of an Argument"

<Source>From the scrolls of the Matsuo-dera monastery, 1174 CE. Commentary on ECMA-262, the chapter on the summoning of functions.</Source>

<Source>A student came to the master and wrote in the sand:</Source>

```js
function gate(a, b, c) {}
gate(1)
```

<Student>"Master, I passed one — but the gate waits for three. Will there be an error?"</Student>

<Action>The master struck him with a stick.</Action>

<Master>"The gate does not close because fewer guests arrived. The empty seats are already occupied — by silence."</Master>

<Student>"So b and c are undefined?"</Student>

<Master>"No. They are mu. They are not defined — but they exist."</Master>

<Haiku>Wind blows — three bowls on the table. One is full.</Haiku>

<Question>If undefined is the absence of value, how does an argument that was never passed differ from one explicitly given undefined?</Question>
