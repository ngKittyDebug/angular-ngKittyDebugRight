# Koan №23 — "On NaN, Which Is Not Equal to Itself"

<Source>Carved on the bottom of a copper alms bowl, Kencho-ji temple, 1953 CE. The bowl had been passed from hand to hand for three hundred years. The inscription was found when the bowl rusted through. Master Asahina said: "At last it has become transparent."</Source>

```js
NaN === NaN   // false
```

<Action>The master struck the student with a stick.</Action>

<Master>"Why do you not ask?"</Master>

<Student>"Because any question about this will be foolish."</Student>

<Master>"At last — wisdom."</Master>

<Action>Long silence.</Action>

<Master>"NaN is not a value. It is an admission of failure. parseInt('stone'). Math.sqrt(-1). You asked for a number — and the world could not answer. NaN is a scar where a number should have been."</Master>

<Student>"How do I check if something is NaN?"</Student>

<Master>"Number.isNaN(). Not bare isNaN() — it lies. Only Number.isNaN() tells the truth."</Master>

<Haiku>A thing without a name. Not equal to itself. Present — and absent.</Haiku>

<Question>NaN is the only entity in JS that denies its own existence. When did you last check the result of a mathematical operation?</Question>
